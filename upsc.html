Prelims

The UPSC Civil Service Prelims exam pattern for 2023:

  • The UPSC CSE Prelims comprises two objective-type papers: General Studies I and General Studies II (CSAT), with a total of 400 marks.
  • Both papers are typically conducted on the same day in two sessions through offline mode (pen-paper).
  • Candidates can expect the Prelims Admit Card to be released on the official UPSC website 2-3 weeks before the exam date.

Here's a breakdown of the UPSC Prelims Exam Pattern:

Exam Paper Number of Questions Negative Marking Duration of Exam Language of Exam Maximum Marks Cut Off Marks
General Studies I 100 Yes (1/3rd of the maximum marks for the question) -0.66 marks for every incorrect answer 2 hours English/Hindi 200 Counted for Merit. Cut-off varies every year.
General Studies II or CSAT 80 Yes (1/3rd of the maximum marks for the question) -0.83 marks for every incorrect answer 2 hours English/Hindi 200 33% qualifying criteria (66 marks)

The official cut-off for the Prelims 2023 will be released post-examination. In the previous Prelims (2021), the cut-off for General category candidates was 87.54.

For a successful Prelims preparation strategy, consider the following:

  • Current Affairs: Aim to cover 12 to 15 months' worth of current affairs. Valuable sources include Yojana Magazine, newspapers (The Hindu/The Indian Express), Press Information Bureau (PIB), and Economic and Political Weekly (EPW).
  • Simultaneous Preparation: Simultaneously prepare for UPSC Prelims and Mains as there is a significant syllabus overlap. Review the complete IAS Syllabus 2023.
  • Practice MCQs: Since Prelims questions are objective-type, extensive practice with MCQs is essential. You can find MCQs for different subjects, including Polity, Indian Economy, General Knowledge, Science and Technology, and Environment and Ecology.

Here is additional information about the UPSC Prelims Syllabus:

General Studies Paper-1 Syllabus:

  • Current events of national and international importance.
  • History of India and Indian National Movement.
  • Indian and World Geography-Physical, Social, Economic Geography of India and the World.
  • Indian Polity and Governance – Constitution, Political System, Panchayati Raj, Public Policy, Rights Issues, etc.
  • Economic and Social Development – Sustainable Development, Poverty, Inclusion, Demographics, Social Sector initiatives, etc.
  • General issues on Environmental Ecology, Biodiversity and Climate Change- that do not require subject specialization.
  • General Science.

General Studies Paper-2 (CSAT) Syllabus:

  • Comprehension.
  • Interpersonal skills including communication skills;
  • Logical reasoning and analytical ability.
  • Decision-making and problem-solving.
  • General mental ability.
  • Basic numeracy (numbers and their relations, orders of magnitude, etc.) (Class X level)
  • Data interpretation (charts, graphs, tables, data sufficiency, etc. – Class X level).
  • English language comprehension skills – Class X level.

Mains

The UPSC Mains examination is a crucial stage in the journey to become an Indian Administrative Service (IAS) officer. It's the second phase of the Civil Services Examination (CSE), which is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC). In this comprehensive guide, we'll delve into various aspects of the UPSC Mains 2023, including the exam pattern, syllabus, preparation strategy, and important details you need to know.

What is the UPSC Mains Exam/CSE (Main)/IAS Mains?

The UPSC Mains exam is the second stage of the Civil Services Examination. IAS aspirants who clear the Prelims, meaning those who score above the cutoff in General Studies Paper I and secure more than 33% marks in General Studies Paper-II (CSAT), are eligible to appear for the UPSC Mains.

Candidates aspiring to become IAS, IPS, IFS, and IRS officers must pass the UPSC Mains before they can proceed to the IAS interview/UPSC Personality Test stage.

How Many Candidates Will Appear for UPSC Mains 2022?

As per UPSC's guidelines, the total number of candidates allowed to appear for UPSC Mains is usually twelve to thirteen times the number of Civil Service vacancies for that exam year. For UPSC 2022, with a total of 1011 vacancies (previously 861), approximately 13,000 candidates are expected to have written the UPSC Mains 2022.

How to Apply for UPSC Mains 2023?

Once the Civil Services Exam (Preliminary) results are declared, successful candidates will have to complete the online Detailed Application Form (DAF) for UPSC Mains on the official website (upsc.gov.in).

The DAF for UPSC Mains consists of six modules/sections:

  1. Personal
  2. Educational Information
  3. Parental Information
  4. Employment Information
  5. Upload Documents
  6. Final Submission

Certain fields like name, date of birth, exam center for UPSC Mains, etc., will be prepopulated in the UPSC Mains DAF based on the information provided during the UPSC online application for CSE (Preliminary).

For UPSC Mains, you must ensure you have the necessary documents for upload. Keep in mind that once the final submission is made, no online changes are allowed. Thus, candidates should be meticulous and double-check all details while filling out the DAF for UPSC Mains. Additionally, it's advisable to keep a copy of the DAF for reference, as it forms the basis for many questions during the IAS interview/UPSC Personality Test stage.

Preparation Strategy for UPSC Mains 2023

Effective preparation for UPSC Mains requires a comprehensive approach. Here's a strategic plan to help you excel in the exam:

  1. Current Affairs: Current Affairs is a crucial part of the UPSC Exam, both for Prelims and Mains. Ideally, aspirants should prepare for Current Affairs for at least 12-15 months. Since the UPSC Mains 2023 is scheduled to be held from September 15, 2023, onwards, candidates should start their Current Affairs preparation from June 2023. Valuable sources for Current Affairs preparation for the IAS exam 2023 include:
    • Yojana Magazine
    • Newspapers (The Hindu/The Indian Express)
    • Press Information Bureau (PIB)
    • Economic and Political Weekly (EPW)

    As part of your IAS preparation, BYJU’S offers comprehensive daily news analysis, summaries of Yojana and EPW, and daily video analysis of The Hindu.

  2. Simultaneous Preparation: Given the significant overlap in syllabus between UPSC Prelims and UPSC Mains, candidates should prepare for both exams simultaneously. This approach ensures a more efficient use of your time and resources.
  3. Answer Writing Practice: Marks in the Civil Services (Main) exam are not awarded for superficial answers. Therefore, candidates should practice answer writing extensively. Consider solving previous year UPSC question papers to develop your writing skills.
  4. Practice with Previous Year Question Papers: Practicing with previous year UPSC question papers is invaluable. It familiarizes you with the exam pattern and helps you understand the type of questions asked. You can download UPSC Previous Year Question Papers from official sources.

UPSC Mains Exam Pattern for the IAS Exam 2023

The UPSC Mains or CSE (Main) pattern was revised in 2015. As per the revised pattern, the UPSC Mains 2023 will consist of nine theory papers (essay descriptive type), but only seven of these will be counted for merit ranking. Additionally, candidates must choose one optional subject for UPSC Mains, which we will explore in more detail later in this article.

The maximum marks for the UPSC Mains exam are 1750 (7 papers * 250 marks each). All papers are three hours in duration. Here's the UPSC Mains pattern for Civil Services Exam 2023:

Important Information Regarding UPSC Mains and UPSC Mains Syllabus:

  • An IAS aspirant's marks in the UPSC Mains and IAS interview determine their final rank in the merit list.
  • UPSC Mains aims to assess the depth of a candidate's knowledge. Marks are only awarded for "relevant, meaningful, and succinct answers."
  • The questions in UPSC Mains test an aspirant's basic understanding, ability to analyze, and their viewpoint on conflicting socio-economic goals, objectives, and demands.
  • Candidates are permitted to use Scientific (Non-Programmable) calculators in UPSC Mains if needed.
  • The examination level for optional subjects in UPSC Mains is equivalent to an honors degree, falling between a bachelor's and master's level. However, for engineering subjects, Law, and Medical Science, the scope is limited to the bachelor's degree level.
  • The UPSC CSE 2023 notification is expected to be released on February 1, 2023. You can download the official UPSC notification for IAS exam 2023 from the UPSC website.

UPSC Mains Syllabus – IAS Exam 2023

The UPSC Mains syllabus can be broadly divided into two parts: Qualifying Papers and Papers Counted for Merit Ranking.

Qualifying Papers:
  • Paper A – Indian Language: This paper assesses candidates' comprehension of given passages, precis writing, usage and vocabulary, short essays, and translation from English to the Indian Language and vice versa. Candidates must score a minimum of 25% marks in Paper A to have their marks in the remaining papers considered. The level of Paper A is akin to Matriculation (or equivalent) level.
  • Paper B – English: Similar to Paper A, Paper B evaluates candidates' comprehension skills, precis writing, usage and vocabulary, and short essays. Again, candidates must secure a minimum of 25% marks in Paper B; otherwise, their marks in other papers will not be declared. The level of Paper B is also Matriculation (or equivalent) level.
Papers Counted for Merit Ranking:
  • Paper I – Essay: In the essay paper, candidates are required to write two essays out of a few given options for a total of 250 marks. Effective and precise expression is crucial, and candidates should keep their essays close to the subject while organizing their ideas orderly.
  • Paper II to Paper V – General Studies (GS) I to GS IV: These papers cover a wide range of subjects, including Indian Heritage and Culture, Governance, Technology, Ethics, History and Geography of the World, Constitution, Economic Development, Integrity, Society, Polity, Environment, Biodiversity, Aptitude, Social Justice, International relations, Security and Disaster Management.

UPSC IAS Interview Overview

The UPSC IAS interview, a pivotal phase in the Civil Services Examination, takes place at the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) headquarters in New Delhi. It spans two sessions each day, beginning at 9:00 AM and 1:00 PM, with specific timing mentioned on the candidate's call letter. The dress code adheres to basic formals: dark trousers with a light-colored plain shirt for men and saris or 'churidar' for women. Despite its relatively lower weightage of 275 marks compared to the Mains, excelling in this interview can significantly impact one's service/cadre allocation.

In 2022, the UPSC offered 1011 vacancies, creating ample opportunities for aspirants to secure their desired positions.

The IAS Interview Process

The IAS interview, more of a discussion than an interrogation, usually lasts around 20 minutes and covers a wide range of topics. Candidates must undergo several preliminary procedures, including document verification and security checks. A government-issued identification is recommended. Bags and mobile phones are not allowed inside, but basic amenities such as tea, water, and biscuits are provided.

Candidates are assigned to panels and sequences, with each panel interviewing 5-6 candidates. The interviews are conducted in a large circular hall. Each interviewee's sequence number determines when they appear before their respective panel. The interviews commence around 10:15 AM for the forenoon session.

Tackling the UPSC interview requires a unique skill set. Confidence, politeness, and a calm demeanor are essential for scoring well, regardless of the interview panel, medium, or question count.

IAS Interview Questions

IAS interview questions are designed to assess mental acuity, general awareness, social etiquette, and overall personality. They go beyond testing theoretical knowledge, emphasizing soft skills. Questions fall into several categories:

  1. Introduction: Candidates should prepare brief, coherent responses for self-introduction, family background, hometown, and name meaning. These answers set the stage for follow-up questions.
  2. Education: Questions may relate to subjects studied during school/graduation, favorite/least favorite subjects, choice of educational institutions, and projects completed. Honesty is crucial, as bluffing is discouraged.
  3. Current Affairs: Candidates are expected to be up-to-date with current headlines and important recent events. Avoid one-dimensional responses unless the question is straightforward.
  4. Work Profile: Questions explore past roles and responsibilities, motivations for joining the civil services, and potential gaps in work history.
  5. Optional Subject: Candidates may be asked why they chose a particular optional subject and why not their graduation subject. Questions might also delve into topics, theories, or contemporary issues related to the chosen optional subject.
  6. Hobbies: Questions about hobbies are tailored to what candidates have listed in their Detailed Application Form (DAF). This could encompass sports, famous sports personalities, or even a practical demonstration of a hobby.

IAS Interview Preparation Strategy

Successful candidates focus on:

  • Staying updated with current affairs.
  • Creating a list of potential interview questions based on their DAF.
  • Practicing responses in front of a mirror or recording themselves.
  • Brushing up on their graduation subject.

The IAS interview is not about knowing every answer but showcasing one's suitability for administrative roles under pressure.

What to Avoid

Candidates should avoid:

  • Stressing out on the eve of the interview.
  • Attempting to bluff their way through questions.
  • Worrying about theoretical queries.
  • Engaging in arguments with panel members and must maintain decorum at all times.

By adopting these strategies and tips, aspirants can enhance their chances of performing well in the UPSC IAS interview and achieving their civil service career goals.

IAS Interview Preparation Tips

Preparing for the IAS interview requires a unique approach. Here are some valuable tips to help you excel:

  • Stay Informed: Keep yourself updated with current affairs and contemporary issues at the national and international levels. Read newspapers, watch news programs, and follow reputable online news sources.
  • Revise Your DAF: Thoroughly review the information you provided in your Detailed Application Form (DAF). Be prepared to answer questions related to your educational background, work experience, and hobbies.
  • Mock Interviews: Consider participating in mock interview sessions. Mock interviews with experienced mentors or coaching institutes can help you practice and receive constructive feedback.
  • Personality Development: Work on enhancing your personality traits. Display confidence, clarity of thought, and a positive attitude during the interview.
  • Prepare for Common Questions: Practice answering common questions such as "Why do you want to become a civil servant?" and "Tell us about yourself." These questions often set the tone for the interview.
  • Optional Subject: Be well-prepared to discuss your chosen optional subject. Familiarize yourself with its core concepts and recent developments.
  • Body Language: Pay attention to your body language. Maintain eye contact with the panel, sit up straight, and speak clearly and confidently.
  • Practice Mock Interviews: Consider participating in mock interview sessions. Mock interviews with experienced mentors or coaching institutes can help you practice and receive constructive feedback.
